Account Pre-Registration & Email Ownership Bypass Leading to Permanent User Lockout

Severity: High / Critical
Category: Authentication • Identity Management • Account Security
Example Target: example.com

The platform allowed users to create accounts using any email address without verifying ownership. No confirmation emails were sent during registration, and the password recovery system failed to deliver reset emails.

This enabled a pre-registration attack, where an attacker could register accounts using other users’ email addresses before the legitimate owner. As a result, attackers could permanently claim email identities and block real users from accessing the platform.

Impact:
• Account pre-registration and identity claiming
• Permanent user lockout (registration, login, and recovery blocked)
• Broken authentication and recovery mechanisms
• Platform trust compromise and potential large-scale abuse

Root Cause:
• No email ownership verification during registration
• Email marked as “used” without validation
• Missing confirmation and password reset email functionality
• No recovery mechanism for unverified accounts

Proof of Concept (Summary):
An attacker registers using a victim’s email address → account is created instantly → no verification required.
When the real user attempts to register or recover the account, access is denied and no reset email is received.
This results in permanent lockout of the legitimate user.
